Lysozyme Production



Producing Lysozyme in E.coli

All protocols with the exception for “Removal of Ivy” and “Immobilization of Lysozyme onto BC” were adapted from the 2022 Calgary iGEM Wetlab protocol folder provided by Anika Zaman. The “Removal of IVY” protocol was adapted from Biovision Incorporated. The “Immobilization of Lysozyme onto BC via Covalent Linkage” protocol was adapted from Ribeiro-Viana et al. (2016).

Restriction Digest

Gene Digest

  1. To a microcentrifuge tube (Tube A), add:
    1. Required amount of malE-Lysozyme-IVY DNA to be digested
    2. 1/10 final total volume of appropriate 10X buffer
    3. 1μl of BlpI restriction enzyme (diluted)
    4. 1μl of PfoI restriction enzyme (diluted)
    5. ddH2O to final volume (10μl)
  2. Centrifuge at 10,000 x g for 5 seconds
  3. Incubate at 37°C for 30 minutes to 3 hours
  4. Heat inactivate restriction enzymes at 82°C for 20 minutes

Plasmid Digest

  1. To a microcentrifuge tube (Tube B), add:
    1. 250ng of pET-16b plasmid DNA to be digested
    2. 2.5ul of NEBuffer 2
    3. 0.5ul of BSA.
    4. 0.5ul of BlpI restriction enzyme (diluted)
    5. 0.5ul of PfoI restriction enzyme (diluted)
    6. ddH2O to final volume (20ul)
  2. Centrifuge at 10,000 x g for 5 seconds
  3. Incubate at 37°C for 30 minutes to 3 hours
  4. Heat inactivate restriction enzymes at 82°C for 20 minutes

Insertion and Ligation

  1. To the first microcentrifuge tube (Tube A), add:
    1. Required amount of linear pET-16b plasmid
    2. 1/10 of total final volume of aliquoted 10X T4 DNA ligase buffer
    3. 1μl of T4 DNA ligase (1U/μl)
    4. ddH2O to final volume (20ul)
  2. Centrifuge at 10,000 x g for 5 seconds
  3. Store at -20°C

Digest Confirmation Via Gel Electrophoresis

  1. Mix 100ml of 1X TAE buffer with 1g of regular or LMP agarose (for a 1% gel). For a small gel, mix 30ml of 1X TAE with 0.3g agarose.
  2. Microwave covered for about 1.5 minutes or until agarose is dissolved.
  3. Cool and add 4μl SYBR safe. For a small gel, add 1.5μl SYBR safe.
  4. Set up a gel tray and balance properly. Pour and cast gel. LMP gels should be cast in the fridge.
  5. Run sample at 100V (regular gel) or 80V (LMP gel) until sample is ¾ of the way down the gel (20-30min).
  6. Image gel.

Transformation of Chemically Competent E. coli

  1. Add ≤ 5μl (≤ 1/10 of the cell aliquot amount) of the malE-Lysozyme-IVY containing pET-16b plasmid DNA sample to a chemically competent cell aliquot. Mix by pipetting gently, then incubate on ice for 30 to 45 minutes.
  2. Heat shock at 42°C for 60-75s.
  3. Incubate on ice for 5 minutes.
  4. Add 250μl of plain LB or SOC media aseptically, then incubate for 30 to 90 minutes at 37°C, shaking.
    • SOC media = more nutrient rich media that may help growth.
  5. Plate 100μl (if big plates) or 50μl (if small or half plates) aseptically.
  6. Incubate plate at 37°C overnight or until growth is observed.

Induction of Protein Expression Via LacI

  1. Make 2-6ml overnights.
  2. In the morning, subculture 2:50 in 50ml or 100ml LB + ampicillin. Incubate at 37°C, shaking until OD600 reaches 0.3 - 0.6 (0.5 is optimal).
  3. Induce with 100mM IPTG (50μl of 1M IPTG into 50ml, 100μl of 1M IPTG into 100ml) and incubate for 8-12 hours at 37°C or 16 hours at 30°C.

Isolation of Protein

Cell Lysis

  1. Centrifuge on max for 5 minutes and discard supernatant (x2 if 100ml culture). Freeze pellet.
  2. Conduct 3x 20 minute freeze-thaw cycles for the pellet at -80°C and 37°C.
  3. Resuspend in 10 ml Resuspension/Equilibration buffer (1X PBS pH 7.4, 300mM NaCl, 10mM imidazole).
  4. Treat with 1mg/ml lysozyme (1ml of 10mg/ml lysozyme with 1% Tween80 into 10ml resuspension) for 30 minutes shaking at 37°C.
  5. Transfer to 15ml falcon tube.
  6. Sonicate (3x of 30A, 5 sec on, 5 sec off, 3 minutes total).
  7. At this point, take a 500μl sample to recover the soluble and insoluble whole cell lysate fraction.
  8. Freeze sonicated samples for 20 minutes or overnight.

Ni-NTA

  1. Take out required number of Ni-NTA columns and let them reach room temperature
  2. Take the top cap off and remove the bottom plug. Place into a 50ml falcon tube and centrifuge at 700g for 5 minutes. Discard flowthrough
  3. Put 10ml of Resuspension buffer into the column and incubate for 10 minutes. Centrifuge at 700g for 5 minutes. Discard flowthrough and replace the bottom plug
  4. Load the 10ml of sonicated sample supernatant onto the columns and replace the top cap. Incubate at 37°C, shaking for 30-60 minutes or longer.
  5. Label new 50ml falcon tubes with ‘post-loading sample’. Remove bottom plug and cap from the columns then place into the 50ml falcon tube. Centrifuge at 700g for 5 minutes and keep the flowthrough if desired.
  6. Perform 3 washes with 10ml Wash buffer (1x PBS pH 7.4, 300mM NaCl, 25mM imidazole). After each wash, centrifuge at 700g for 5 minutes and collect the wash samples if desired
  7. Add 4ml of Elution buffer (1x PBS pH 7.4, 300mM NaCl, 250mM imidazole) to the column, and incubate for at least 5 minutes. Centrifuge at 700g for 5 minutes and label collected fraction “Elution fraction 1”

Removal of IVY

  1. To 10 μl of target protein in an Eppendorf tube add 0.05-0.5 μg (0.5-5 mU) of TEV protease.
  2. Add 10 μl of freshly prepared 50 mM Tris, buffer, 0.1 M NaCl, pH 8.0 containing 5 mM DTT. Mix well by pipetting up and down. Incubate at 34°C for 1 h. Analyze by SDS-PAGE.
  3. After completion of cleavage reaction, TEV can be removed by passing the entire mixture through Ni-chelating resin. Collect the flow through and wash with the resin with 50 mM Tris, buffer, 0.1 M NaCl, pH 8.0. Combine the washes with the flow through to obtain cleaved protein product.

SDS-PAGE Gel

Preparing SDS-PAGE Gels

  1. Clean the glass plates thoroughly with methanol, then set up pouring apparatus.
  2. For one 12% resolving gel, combine the following:
    • 1.6ml milliQ water
    • 1.25ml 1.5M Tris buffer, pH 8.8
    • 50μl 10% SDS
    • 2ml 30% 29:1 Acrylamide:Bis-acrylamide
    • 50μl Ammonium Persulfate (APS)
    • 5μl TEMED
  3. Pour gel and add 99% isopropanol to the top. Let set completely.
  4. Pour off isopropanol and rinse with ddH2O. Use a kimwipe to completely dry between the plates.
  5. For one 6% stacking gel, combine the following:
    • 680μl milliQ water
    • 130μl 1M Tris buffer, pH 6.8
    • 10μl 10% SDS
    • 170μl 30% 29:1 Acrylamide:Bis-acrylamide
    • 10μl Ammonium Persulfate (APS)
    • 1μl TEMED
  6. Pour gel and insert comb. Let set completely.

Preparing Protein Samples

  1. Mix an aliquot of the protein sample with an appropriate amount of 4x SDS-PAGE loading buffer (200mM Tris-Cl pH 6.8, 8% SDS, 40% glycerol, 0.4% bromophenol blue) to get a final concentration of 1x SDS-PAGE loading buffer.
    • Add 1% β-Mercaptoethanol if required.
  2. Boil samples for 2 minutes and spin down.

Running Gels

  1. Set up SDS-PAGE apparatus with the comb-side of the gels facing the inner chamber.
  2. Fill inner chamber completely with 1x SDS-PAGE running buffer (1x Tris-Glycine, 0.1% SDS), and outer chamber halfway.
    • 1x SDS-PAGE running buffer can be reused approximately 4-5 times.
  3. Load standards and samples.
  4. Run for 10 minutes at 100V, followed by 30 minutes at 180V.

Staining Gels

  1. Remove gel from plates and place in a box with enough Coomassie Blue staining solution (0.25% Coomassie R-250, 40% methanol, 10% glacial acetic acid) to cover the gel. Shake at room temperature for 45 minutes.
  2. Destain the gel by replacing the Coomassie Blue staining solution with destaining solution (40% methanol, 10% glacial acetic acid) and shaking at room temperature for 10-30 minutes.
  3. Repeat destaining step as necessary until distinct bands can be visualized.
  4. The gel can be stored in dH2O at room temperature for a few weeks.

Immobilization of Lysozyme onto BC via Covalent Linkage

  1. Prepare anhydride succinic by combining 4g succinic acid to 6.4ml acetic anhydride in a round bottom flask, and allow the reaction to occur for 1 hour at 100oC, then at room temperature for 2 hours, and finally at 8oC for 30 minutes.
  2. Wash BC membranes with methanol 3 times for 5 minutes each, and wash with dichloromethane for 5 minutes to remove water. Repeat two more times.
  3. Place BC membranes in 10 mL of dichloromethane, 174 μL of pyridine and 216 mg of succinic anhydride, refluxed at 70oC for 24 hours.
  4. Wash BC membranes with distilled water 3 times, then wash with 3X ethanol.
  5. Dry succinylated BC membranes at room temperature.
  6. Combine 0.035g of succinylated BC membrane with 8ml of anhydrous DMF, 40mg of 3-(ethyliminomethyleneamino)-N,N-dimethylpropan-1-amine (EDC) and 8mg of 4-dimethylaminopyridine (DMAP).
  7. Let the reaction sit at room temperature for 1 hour, then add 0.08 mol of lysozyme protein per mol of cellulose/EDC/NHS and agitated in a tumbling table for 21 hours.
  8. Wash the succinylated BC membrane 3 times with distilled water and dry at room temperature in a vacuum desiccator.
