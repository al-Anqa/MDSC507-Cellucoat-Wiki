58 percent of food produced in Canada is wasted every year.

In fact, food waste costs the average Canadian household over $1700 a year, with more than 4 million Canadians experiencing food insecurity. Considering all of this, the majority of our food is still being thrown away.

Because of the fresh produce needing to be shipped great distances, the bumpy and extended transport compromises its quality through bruising and rotting. Current preservation methods used to combat these obstacles – including freezing and drying of fruits and vegetables – often further limit these communities’ access to quality fresh foods.

To minimize food waste and build a more sustainable and equitable society, we present Cellucoat.

Our project brings together a multidisciplinary team with students from iGEM Calgary and a strong wealth of knowledge from researchers and professionals that combines synthetic biology with engineering techniques and technologies to develop a novel solution to this issue.

Our aim is to sustainably reduce food waste, improve food preservation, and improve food accessibility through producing an edible and environmentally friendly bacterial cellulose coating that is applied directly to fruit to protect it from premature rotting and reduce physical damage.
